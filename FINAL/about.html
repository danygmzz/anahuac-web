about me.

Do you want to know more about me? Cool. I don't think I'm a particularly

interesting person but I'll leave you to it.

who am i? who am i? 24601.

if you caught that reference, we are now friends forever. sorry, i don't make the rules. and now that we're friends forever, i should probably introduce myself: i'm daniela, i'm 21 years old, and i'm a graphic designer. on my down time, i'm also an aspiring illustrator and 2d animator.

i grew up in mexico city amongst a herd of graphic and indsutrial designers exclusively: my dad is a designer, most of my uncles are designers, and my older sister is a designer. i sometimes like to think i carry it in my blood, like a creativity gene that just won't let me rest until i push out whatever my brain is trying to come up with. needless to say, my choice to study multimedia design during my time at university came as no surprise to my family and was greatly encouraged, something that not many people are privileged enough to say. we're all aware that artists go hungry sometimes.

either way, i started my career studies at Universidad Anáhuac México in 2017, and just finished my junior year of university. i work on my free time: i got a job as a research assistant for a psychologist i met during high school, which doesn't necessarily add to my experience as a designer but it's taught me a ton of things about life, psychology itself, and most importantly research and information studies. this means i'm resourceful and fast, and extremely thorough whenever i research, which IS something that works when creating something, so my job is great despite everything. i wouldn't personally recommend having a job and homework at the same time, though. it isn't fun. avoid at all costs if that's something you can do.

i've still got an entire year of university to go, but i'm more excited than ever about what the future holds for me. for the first time in my life, i'm doing things that i actually like and that i can genuinely see myself doing at some point in the near future. i've lost countless hours of sleep and a ton of money on design and illustration tools, but it's all going to be worth it in the end, and i couldn't be happier about it.
